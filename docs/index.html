Independance

Communication

Responsibility

What I learnt

The expedition to Kenya ignited a flame within me, kindling a passion for conservation that transcends mere interest—it's become a calling. As I stand on the precipice of a gap year, my aspirations soar towards making a tangible difference in the world. Whether my efforts ripple out in grand waves or gentle whispers, the aim is to sow seeds of change that germinate into visible gratitude from those whose lives I touch. This journey has planted the possibility that what starts as a temporary pursuit could blossom into a lifelong vocation. One conviction stands unshakable: my commitment to the stewardship of nature and the safeguarding of its creatures will remain unwavering. The path ahead may be uncharted, but the direction is clear—I am dedicated to contributing to the preservation and protection of the natural world, a pledge that I intend to uphold with every fiber of my being.

The journey to Kenya also ignited a voracious flame of curiosity within me, a desire to immerse myself in the rich tapestry of the world's cultures. I find myself yearning to savor new cuisines, to delve into unfamiliar traditions, to absorb the essence of life as experienced by the myriad tribes and communities that paint our world with their diverse hues of existence. These cultures, each with their unique rhythms of living in harmony, offer a glimpse into a life I aspire to understand and perhaps emulate in my own quest for balance and fulfillment.

This newfound zeal for exploration refuses to be confined; it propels me towards a horizon brimming with the promise of discovery. The coming years of my life are poised to become a mosaic of learning, each piece a fragment of cultural history, culinary adventures, and traditional wisdom collected from the corners of the earth. My journey is no longer about merely moving through spaces but about allowing each experience to transform me, enriching my understanding of the world and its inhabitants. With every step, I intend to weave these insights into the fabric of my being, ensuring that my life is a perpetual celebration of the diversity and beauty that our planet has to offer.

Working at a nearby school

The nature surrounding the camp

I would’ve always thought that Kenya would be a dry area but that is very not true in regard to areas by the ocean where Camp Firefly was! The trees and bushes surrounding us were so green and succulent radiating with life and fresh oxygen.
